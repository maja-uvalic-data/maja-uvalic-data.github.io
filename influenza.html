Influenza Season

Exploratory and time-series analysis of influenza activity over time, combining epidemiological data with external factors to better understand seasonal patterns.

Excel Power Point Tableau
View on GitHub View on Tableau

Project Overview

The US influenza season leads to increased patient loads, especially among vulnerable populations. This project helps a medical staffing agency plan temporary staff across all 50 states by analyzing influenza trends, determining when and how many staff are needed. Excel is used for data analysis and PowerPoint for visualization, enabling proactive workforce planning for the upcoming flu season.

Key Questions

  • Identify seasonal peaks in influenza activity across the United States.
  • Compare influenza levels across different time periods and geographic regions.
  • Estimate how many healthcare staff are needed in each state during peak influenza periods.
  • Explore whether influenza levels and mortality differ by age group, especially among people aged 65 and older.

Data & Tools

The analysis uses publicly available data to understand influenza trends and staffing needs across the United States:

  • Influenza deaths by geography – data from the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC).
  • Population data by geography, time, age, and gender – data from the US Census Bureau.

Tools:

  • Excel – data cleaning, aggregation, and preliminary analysis.
  • Tableau – interactive visualizations and time-series exploration.
  • PowerPoint – creating slides and communicating results to stakeholders.

Methodology

  1. Performed comprehensive data cleaning and standardization, including unifying date formats and preparing the dataset for time-based analysis.
  2. Conducted statistical comparisons across years and seasons to evaluate significant differences in influenza activity and identify meaningful patterns.
  3. Used Tableau to develop clear visual narratives, applying trend lines, rolling averages, and seasonal comparisons to highlight anomalies and support data-driven interpretation.

Key Insights

  • As the number of elderly people (65+) increases, influenza mortality rises accordingly.

  • Influenza activity typically peaks during the winter months.

  • California, New York, and Texas show the highest mortality rates, indicating the need for increased medical staffing during flu season.

Recommendations

  • Who is most at risk?

    Individuals aged 65 and older are a high-risk population.

  • Where to send medical staff?

    The most vulnerable states are California, New York, Texas, Florida, and Pennsylvania.

  • When to act?

    Target the flu season peak, typically from December to March.
