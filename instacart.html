Instacart Basket Analysis

Exploratory data analysis of online grocery shopping patterns to help Instacart better understand customer behavior and optimize marketing strategies.

Python Pandas Seaborn Matplotlib Data Cleaning EDA
View on GitHub View Jupyter Notebook

Project Overview

This project is part of the CareerFoundry Data Analytics program. The goal was to analyze Instacart’s anonymized customer orders and identify patterns in purchasing behavior, such as: when customers shop, which products are frequently bought together, and which customer segments can be targeted with specific marketing campaigns.

Business Questions

  • Which products are ordered most frequently?
  • How do order patterns differ by time of day and day of week?
  • Are there specific customer segments that behave differently?
  • Which departments and product categories generate the most sales?

Data & Tools

The Instacart dataset consists of several CSV files with millions of rows containing information about orders, products, aisles, and departments. I used Python and the following libraries:

  • Pandas – data cleaning, joining, aggregation
  • NumPy – numerical operations
  • Seaborn & Matplotlib – visualizations
  • Jupyter Notebook – analysis and documentation

Methodology

  1. Imported and inspected all CSV files
  2. Cleaned the data (missing values, data types, duplicates)
  3. Merged tables into a single analysis-ready dataframe
  4. Created new features (e.g., order_hour_of_day, order_frequency)
  5. Analyzed customer behavior and product demand
  6. Visualized patterns with bar charts, histograms, and crosstabs

Key Insights

  • The majority of orders are placed in the late afternoon and early evening, suggesting that marketing campaigns could be timed around these peak hours.
  • A small number of departments (e.g., Produce, Dairy & Eggs) account for a large share of total orders.
  • There are clear patterns in reorders, indicating that Instacart can recommend recurring items and personalize the shopping experience.

Conclusion & Next Steps

The analysis provides a foundation for targeted marketing campaigns and personalized recommendations. In the future, the project could be extended with customer segmentation using clustering methods or predictive models for forecasting next likely orders.
