Instacart Basket Analysis

Exploratory data analysis of online grocery shopping patterns to help Instacart better understand customer behavior and optimize marketing strategies.

Python Jupyter Notebook Power Point
View on GitHub

Project Overview

Instacart is an online grocery store that operates through an app. While the company already has strong sales, they are looking to gain deeper insights into customer purchasing patterns. The goal of this project is to perform an data analysis of Instacart’s data in order to uncover customer segments and shopping behaviors. These insights will help inform a targeted marketing strategy, ensuring that Instacart can reach the right customer groups with the most relevant products.

Most orders are placed in the late afternoon and early evening, especially on weekends.

Mid-range products (€5–15) represent the majority of sales, indicating customers prefer moderately priced items.

The most popular department is Dairy & Eggs, followed by Produce, Beverages, Frozen, and Canned Goods.

In all four regions (Midwest, Northeast, South, and West), low spenders dominate. High spenders represent only 21–22%.

For all customer profiles, the main shopping days are Saturday and Sunday — except for Young Adults, whose peak days are Friday and Sunday.

Methodology

  1. Imported and inspected all CSV files
  2. Cleaned the data (missing values, data types, duplicates)
  3. Merged tables into a single analysis-ready dataframe
  4. Created new features (e.g., order_hour_of_day, order_frequency)
  5. Analyzed customer behavior and product demand
  6. Visualized patterns with bar charts, histograms, and heatmaps

Key Insights

  • Most orders are placed in the late afternoon and early evening, especially on weekends.

  • Mid-range products (€5–15) represent the majority of sales, indicating that customers primarily favor moderately priced items.

  • The most popular department is Dairy & Eggs, followed by Produce, Beverages, Frozen, and Canned Goods.

  • In all four regions (Midwest, Northeast, South, and West), low spenders dominate. High spenders represent between 21 and 22%. Variation between regions is very small.

  • For all customer profiles, the main shopping days are Saturday and Sunday, except for Young Adults, whose peak days are Friday and Sunday.

Recommendations

  • The busiest periods are Saturday and Sunday from 9 AM to 4 PM, suggesting that marketing campaigns should be scheduled during these peak hours to maximize visibility.
  • Highest spending: 1AM–6AM (with some dips). Promote higher-priced products during these hours when customers are more willing to spend.
  • Top-selling departments: Dairy & Eggs, followed by Produce and Beverages. Focus marketing on these categories to boost sales and attract customers.
  • Dairy & Eggs peak on weekends. Focus promotions on families and tailor offers to customer profiles.
